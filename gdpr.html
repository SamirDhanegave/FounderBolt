GDPR Compliance

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) Information

Last updated: December 2, 2025

Our Commitment to GDPR Compliance

At FounderBolt, we are fully committed to complying with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and protecting the privacy rights of all individuals within the European Union and European Economic Area.

GDPR Overview: The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a comprehensive data protection law that came into effect on May 25, 2018. It grants EU citizens control over their personal data and simplifies the regulatory environment for international business.

Your GDPR Rights

Under GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Right to Access

You have the right to obtain confirmation as to whether or not personal data concerning you is being processed.

Right to Rectification

You have the right to have inaccurate personal data rectified, or completed if it is incomplete.

Right to Erasure

You have the right to have your personal data erased in certain circumstances ("right to be forgotten").

Right to Restrict Processing

You have the right to request the restriction or suppression of your personal data.

Right to Data Portability

You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used format.

Right to Object

You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.

Data Processing Principles

We adhere to the following GDPR data processing principles:

Principle Description Our Implementation
Lawfulness, Fairness & Transparency Processing must be lawful, fair, and transparent to the data subject We provide clear privacy notices and obtain consent where required
Purpose Limitation Data must be collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes We clearly state the purpose of data collection in our privacy policy
Data Minimization Only necessary data should be collected for the stated purposes We collect only the data essential for providing our services
Accuracy Data must be kept accurate and up to date We provide mechanisms for users to update their information
Storage Limitation Data should not be kept longer than necessary We have data retention policies and regularly review stored data
Integrity & Confidentiality Data must be processed securely We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
Accountability The controller is responsible for demonstrating compliance We maintain documentation of our data processing activities

Legal Basis for Processing

We process personal data based on one or more of the following legal bases:

  • Consent: You have given clear consent for us to process your personal data for a specific purpose
  • Contract: Processing is necessary for a contract we have with you
  • Legal Obligation: Processing is necessary for us to comply with the law
  • Legitimate Interests: Processing is necessary for our legitimate interests, provided your interests don't override ours

Withdrawing Consent: Where we rely on consent as the legal basis for processing, you have the right to withdraw your consent at any time. This will not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

International Data Transfers

As a global company, FounderBolt may transfer personal data to countries outside the EU/EEA. When we do so, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place:

  • Adequacy decisions by the European Commission
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  • Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
  • Certification under the EU-US Data Privacy Framework

Data Breach Procedures

We have implemented procedures to deal with any suspected personal data breach:

  1. Immediate assessment of the breach scope and impact
  2. Containment and recovery measures
  3. Notification to the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours where required
  4. Communication to affected individuals when the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms
